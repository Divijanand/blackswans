## Event 10: Quantum Computing Announcements (2024–2026) — The Anticipated Shock

### 1. Vectors Hit
* **Tech Discontinuity:** A slow-burn paradigm shift from Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum (NISQ) devices to fault-tolerant logical computing. Google's Willow chip (Dec 2024) and Microsoft's Majorana 2 (June 2026) announcements prove that exponential error reduction and practical qubit stability (lasting seconds instead of microseconds) are actively being solved.
* **Geopolitical / Security:** The looming threat of "Q-Day" (when a quantum computer can break RSA encryption). It supercharges the "Store Now, Decrypt Later" intelligence-gathering strategy among nation-states, weaponizing data that is currently secure.
* **Financial:** Creates a latent overhang on the classical AI CapEx narrative. If quantum systems prove capable of drastically accelerating AI optimization and materials science, the trillions invested in classical GPU data centers risk early obsolescence for specific frontier workloads.

### 2. Key Actors Affected
* **Felt it first:** National intelligence agencies and the global cryptography sector. Institutions like NIST were forced to finalize and mandate Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) standards years before a fully viable machine existed, purely to defend against the anticipated shock.
* **Felt it hardest:** The blockchain and crypto ecosystems. The theoretical realization that a million-qubit fault-tolerant machine could instantly derive private keys from public addresses poses an existential, zero-day threat to the entire decentralized finance space.
* **Insulated:** General enterprise SaaS and standard consumer technology. Because quantum computers do not replace classical processors for standard binary logic (email, web hosting, basic computing), the broad software economy remains insulated from the physical hardware disruption.

### 3. Precursor Signals
* **Google's Sycamore (2019):** Demonstrated the first highly contested milestone of "quantum supremacy" on a narrow random circuit sampling task.
* **Google's Willow Chip (December 2024):** A profound breakthrough where adding qubits actually *reduced* the error rate—crossing the critical threshold required to build massive, scalable systems without compounding noise.
* **Microsoft's Majorana 2 (June 2026):** Transitioned topological qubits from analog to digital control, achieving a 1,000x stability improvement over its predecessor and dramatically shortening the timeline to a commercial quantum supercomputer.

### 4. Shock Duration
* **Decades of Slow-Burn leading to an Instantaneous Break.** Unlike the sudden drop of ChatGPT or a stock crash, this is a "phantom shock." The industry is absorbing massive, rolling R&D costs over decades. But the moment a Cryptographically Relevant Quantum Computer (CRQC) successfully runs Shor’s algorithm against live encryption, the global financial shock will occur in minutes.

### 5. Recurrence & Swan Classification
* **Grey Swan (Technology) / Black Swan (Timing).** The arrival of practical quantum computing is the most heavily telegraphed disruption in modern science. The physics, the scaling laws, and the roadmaps are fully public (a White/Grey Swan). However, the precise Tuesday morning when a sovereign actor or private lab actually turns on a machine capable of decrypting the global banking system will trigger a Black Swan market panic.
