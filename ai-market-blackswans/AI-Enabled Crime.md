## Event 12: AI-Enabled Crime & Synthetic Fraud (2023–Present) — The Trust Discontinuity

*(Note: This functions as the new 7th vector—Weaponization. It covers the systemic rise of deepfake financial scams, including the highly publicized corporate and retail incidents you flagged.)*

### 1. Vectors Hit
* **Tech Discontinuity (Weaponization):** The collapse of biometric and sensory trust. The fundamental assumption that live audio and video serve as cryptographic proof of identity was completely broken by real-time generative models.
* **Financial:** Direct, asymmetric extraction of capital. Scammers entirely bypassed trillion-dollar cybersecurity firewalls by attacking the human layer directly (e.g., using synthetic media to authorize massive wire transfers or manipulate retail stock purchases).
* **Regulatory / Security:** Forces an immediate, global overhaul of corporate compliance protocols, KYC (Know Your Customer) infrastructure, and the legal frameworks surrounding personality rights and digital identity theft.

### 2. Key Actors Affected
* **Felt it first:** Corporate finance departments and retail investors. Scammers used deepfakes of prominent figures to extract funds directly—bypassing traditional "hacking" entirely.
* **Felt it hardest:** The cybersecurity and cyber-insurance industries. They were suddenly faced with underwriting "technology-enhanced social engineering" claims where the victim willingly transferred the money and no internal IT systems were technically breached.
* **Insulated:** Purely zero-trust algorithmic transaction systems (like blockchain smart contracts). Because they rely entirely on cryptographic keys and mathematical proofs rather than human authorization or biometric verification, they are functionally immune to deepfake social engineering.

### 3. Precursor Signals
* **Early Audio Cloning (2019):** A highly publicized case where criminals used early AI voice-cloning to impersonate the CEO of a UK energy firm's parent company, tricking a subordinate into transferring $243,000.
* **The Democratization of Generation (2023):** The explosion of cheap, consumer-grade voice cloning (like ElevenLabs) and open-source video face-swapping models. The tooling transitioned from state-level intelligence labs to low-skill scammers in less than 24 months.
* **Phishing Maturation:** The decades-long history of Business Email Compromise (BEC) scams clearly indicated that once attackers had the tools to upgrade from text to video, they would immediately do so.

### 4. Shock Duration
* **Permanent / Structural.** This is not a shock that mean-reverts. It represents a permanent degradation of the digital trust environment. We are currently in the messy transition period where society must adopt new "post-truth" verification standards (like cryptographic watermarking and mandatory multi-factor authentication for human-to-human corporate calls).

### 5. Recurrence & Swan Classification
* **Grey Swan.** Social engineering, impersonation, and fraud are as old as commerce itself. The threat was entirely known. The shock was the *fidelity, accessibility, and scale*—the realization that a teenager with a laptop could convincingly impersonate a Fortune 500 CEO on a live video call.
