## Event 5: NVIDIA Reaching $1T Valuation (May 2023) — The Supply-Chain Repricing

### 1. Vectors Hit
* **Supply-Chain:** Exposed the absolute global choke point of semiconductor manufacturing, specifically TSMC’s advanced packaging capabilities (CoWoS - Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate). Compute instantly became a scarce, non-elastic physical commodity.
* **Financial:** Initiated a structural macro-realignment of corporate capital expenditure (CapEx). Billions of dollars were systematically diverted away from legacy data center infrastructure (CPUs) into accelerated AI infrastructure (GPUs).
* **Tech Discontinuity:** Codified the hardware-software stack dominance of CUDA. The market realized that NVIDIA was not just a hardware manufacturer, but a software-monopolized tollbooth for all modern computation.

### 2. Key Actors Affected
* **Felt it first:** Tier-1 Cloud Service Providers (Microsoft AWS, Google, Meta). They entered a desperate, hyper-competitive bidding war to secure allocations of H100 GPUs, effectively turning their executive teams into supply-chain procurement agents.
* **Felt it hardest:** Mid-tier AI startups and academic research labs that were priced out or backlogged for over a year, inducing a severe state of "compute poverty" that dictated who could build frontier models. Also legacy hardware rivals (Intel, AMD) who found themselves generationally behind.
* **Insulated:** TSMC and advanced packaging equipment vendors (like ASML and Besi). As the sole manufacturers capable of producing these silicon chips, they were mathematically guaranteed to win regardless of which software company or cloud provider eventually monetized the workloads.

### 3. Precursor Signals
* **The Q1 2024 Earnings Guidance (May 2023):** NVIDIA shocked Wall Street by forecasting a historic 64% revenue jump for the upcoming quarter ($11B vs. $7.2B expected), signaling an immediate structural break from traditional linear growth models.
* **The Crypto-Mining Eras (2017 & 2021):** Twice before, the global supply chain had collapsed under sudden parallel-compute demand shocks. Anyone analyzing the data could see that GPUs were highly sensitive to software-driven paradigm shifts.
* **Jensen Huang’s 2016 Hand-Delivery:** The highly publicized moment when NVIDIA’s CEO personally delivered the first DGX-1 AI supercomputer to OpenAI. The strategic bet on deep learning hardware had been openly visible for nearly a decade.

### 4. Shock Duration
* **Years.** The compute constraint and the scramble for silicon dictated the entire macroeconomic narrative of 2023, 2024, and 2025. The shock only began to yield to a new equilibrium as massive CoWoS capacity expansions came online and alternative silicon architectures matured.

### 5. Recurrence & Swan Classification
* **Grey Swan.** The compute demands required to run transformer models were entirely predictable via established scaling laws. However, the *velocity* at which global hyperscalers simultaneously decided to entirely scrap their data center deployment roadmaps to prioritize AI compute was a black-swan-like structural narrative break for public equity markets.
