## Event 8: Chip Export Controls (October 2022 – March 2025) — The "Drip" Shock

### 1. Vectors Hit
* **Regulatory / Policy:** The weaponization of the US Commerce Department’s Entity List and Foreign Direct Product Rule (FDPR) to systematically cut off an adversary's access to frontier technology.
* **Geopolitical:** A hard decoupling of the US and Chinese tech sectors, triggering an all-out, government-backed effort by Beijing to achieve semiconductor self-sufficiency.
* **Supply-Chain:** The physical rewiring of global hardware flows, leading to extreme geographic supply imbalances, localized price spikes, and the massive expansion of an international silicon black market.

### 2. Key Actors Affected
* **Felt it first:** Pure-play hardware manufacturers like ASML. Caught in the geopolitical crossfire, ASML suffered a textbook distribution shift—shedding over $50 billion in market cap (a 16% single-day plunge) in late 2024 when export restrictions crushed their 2025 China bookings outlook.
* **Felt it hardest:** The Chinese AI ecosystem (facing workforce reductions and hardware starvation) and global logistics networks. It forced the creation of complex smuggling rings, such as the network that funneled $390 million worth of Dell and Supermicro servers loaded with banned NVIDIA GPUs through Malaysia.
* **Insulated:** US domestic hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP). The export controls artificially trapped the world's supply of advanced silicon within the borders of the US and its closest allies, guaranteeing domestic compute abundance.

### 3. Precursor Signals
* **Early ASML Restrictions (2019):** The US successfully pressured the Dutch government to block the sale of ASML’s most advanced Extreme Ultraviolet (EUV) lithography machines to China years before the generative AI boom.
* **The CHIPS and Science Act (August 2022):** Signaled the US government's formal transition from a passive free-trade semiconductor policy to an active, heavily subsidized industrial containment strategy.
* **The Huawei Blueprint:** The Trump administration’s successful crippling of Huawei's 5G dominance via export controls provided the exact legislative and strategic template used for the AI hardware bans.

### 4. Shock Duration
* **Rolling (Years).** This is the definitive "Drip Shock." Rather than a single mean-reversion event, the shock arrived in a sequence of escalating clamps (October 2022, October 2023, December 2024, and the Trump administration's March 2025 entity blacklisting). Each iteration destroyed the previous equilibrium and forced the market to instantly price in new routing and compliance costs.

### 5. Recurrence & Swan Classification
* **Grey Swan.** Using export controls to choke a rival’s military-technological advancement is a historical standard (e.g., the Cold War COCOM lists). The shock factor wasn't the policy itself, but the *financial market whiplash* (the suddenness of ASML's plunge) and the sheer scale of the secondary black markets that emerged to bypass the bans.
