## Event 14: NVIDIA Blackwell & CUDA Moat (2024–2026) — The CapEx Scarcity Shock

### 1. Vectors Hit
* **Tech Discontinuity:** By packaging two reticle-limited dies with a 10 TB/s chip-to-chip interconnect, NVIDIA bypassed the physical reticle size limits of monolithic silicon. Blackwell packed 208 billion transistors (compared to Hopper's 80 billion), effectively sustaining Moore's Law through advanced packaging and fundamentally shifting the baseline for frontier AI training.
* **Supply-Chain:** The sheer demand created absolute scarcity. Blackwell capacity was sold out through the end of 2025 before the chips even physically shipped, removing float from the market and turning computing power into a hoarded commodity.
* **Financial:** A brutal consolidation of pricing power. Four hyperscale customers accounted for roughly 61% of NVIDIA's data-center revenue. They pre-paid billions to lock in capacity, fundamentally out-bidding smaller competitors and driving up NVIDIA's gross margins to software-like levels.

### 2. Key Actors Affected
* **Felt it first:** Mega-cap tech (Meta, Microsoft, Google, Amazon). They had to immediately adjust their corporate structures and Wall Street guidance to account for $50B+ annual CapEx run-rates just to secure their place in the Blackwell queue.
* **Felt it hardest:** Mid-tier AI labs, startups, and sovereign clouds. The absolute lack of supply forced them to redesign their business models around "compute poverty," relying on quantized models or waiting a year to access state-of-the-art infrastructure.
* **Insulated:** NVIDIA itself. Its CUDA software moat—bundling networking, optimization, and developer productivity—ensured that buyers couldn't easily pivot to cheaper AMD or Intel chips simply based on price-per-FLOP, cementing the lock-in.

### 3. Precursor Signals
* **The Hopper (H100) Squeeze (2023):** The immediate precursor was the supply bottleneck of the previous generation. The market already knew that whoever secured hardware dictated the AI frontier.
* **Advanced Packaging Constraints:** TSMC's CoWoS packaging limits were heavily reported on in 2023, telegraphing that the next generation of chips would be bottlenecked not by silicon design, but by complex physical assembly limits.

### 4. Shock Duration
* **Years (Structural).** This wasn't a sudden drop, but the establishment of a multi-year supply ceiling. The scarcity shock will last until either alternative silicon (like custom ASICs from hyperscalers) matures or TSMC vastly expands its advanced packaging capacity in 2026/2027.

### 5. Recurrence & Swan Classification
* **Grey Rhino.** A monopoly leveraging an architectural moat to maintain extreme pricing power during a demand spike is entirely predictable. The shock was the *scale* of the capital outlay—hyper-scalers willingly engaging in the largest synchronized infrastructure build-out in human history.
