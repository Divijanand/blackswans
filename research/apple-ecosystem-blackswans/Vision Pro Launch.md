## Event 6: Vision Pro Launch & Developer Response (Feb 2024) — The Spatial Incubation

### 1. Vectors Hit
* **Tech Discontinuity:** A fundamental leap in human-computer interaction (HCI). It transitioned UI from 2D multi-touch screens to 3D spatial computing, mandating entirely new developer design primitives based on eye-tracking, hand-gestures, and volumetric rendering (via RealityKit and visionOS).
* **Financial:** The staggering $3,500 entry price artificially suppressed the Total Addressable Market (TAM), breaking the traditional consumer-developer flywheel. It forced studios to justify massive R&D costs against an inherently microscopic active user base.
* **Supply-Chain:** Hard production limits dictated by the complexity of the 4K micro-OLED displays (manufactured by Sony), enforcing an artificial cap on how quickly the hardware footprint could physically scale.

### 2. Key Actors Affected
* **Felt it first:** Early adopter developers, indie studios, and spatial design agencies who had to completely relearn UI/UX, abandoning flat grids for complex depth and physical ergonomics.
* **Felt it hardest:** The immediate consumer ecosystem. Apple suffered a profound "developer boycott" at launch from tech incumbents—Netflix, YouTube, and Spotify all conspicuously refused to build native apps. This forced users to rely on clunky Safari wrappers and severely chilled early consumer momentum.
* **Insulated / Beneficiaries:** Enterprise, Medical, and B2B developers (e.g., Stryker, Siemens Healthineers). In high-end surgical planning or architectural visualization, a $3,500 headset is a trivial expense compared to legacy enterprise equipment. These sectors insulated themselves from consumer friction and quietly thrived on the hardware's extreme fidelity.

### 3. Precursor Signals
* **ARKit and LiDAR Integration (2017–2020):** Apple spent seven years seeding augmented reality frameworks and depth sensors into iPhones and iPads, stealthily training its developer base in 3D object placement long before the headset existed.
* **The Acquisition Trail:** A decade-long breadcrumb trail of Apple acquiring niche AR/VR hardware and software startups (NextVR, Vrvana, Akonia Holographics).
* **The Meta Quest Playbook:** Meta’s billions burned on Reality Labs clearly signaled the sheer difficulty, low retention rates, and immense capital required to drag a mainstream consumer base into a headset ecosystem.

### 4. Shock Duration
* **Years (Slow Burn / Incubation).** This was not an overnight revolution like the iPhone App Store launch. After the initial February 2024 hype spike of roughly 600 native apps, the developer response settled into a multi-year "incubation winter." Throughout 2025 and 2026, the ecosystem stabilized into a slow, methodical trickle of native apps as the industry collectively waits for Apple to solve the weight and price friction with a future, mass-market non-Pro headset.

### 5. Recurrence & Swan Classification
* **Grey Swan.** A highly rumored, heavily patented Apple headset was arguably the least surprising product launch in tech history. The actual shock was the *developer ambivalence*. Apple's ecosystem is famously obedient and lucrative; seeing top-tier application incumbents aggressively refuse to participate on day one was a stark, unprecedented fracture in Apple's "build it and they will come" aura.

### Framework Scoring

* **Δξ (Impact): 6.0/10** — It successfully established the premium baseline for spatial computing but failed to trigger an immediate, macroeconomic software boom due to immense price and form-factor friction.
* **𝒩 (Novelty): 8.5/10** — The pure technical execution (sub-12ms photon-to-photon latency, flawless eye-tracking, and high-res real-time passthrough) achieved a level of biometric and hardware integration previously thought to be years away.
* **ℛ (Relevance): 8.0/10** — Essential for testing the framework's ability to measure a *stalled or elongated* tech discontinuity. It proves that even when the technology is functionally flawless, a high financial barrier combined with missing key actors (the incumbent developer boycott) can severely delay the realization of a structural shock.
