# Domain-Agnostic Macroeconomic Shock Classifier

This framework provides an objective, repeatable decision procedure to filter out high-drama market noise and classify genuine structural disruptions. It is designed to be completely domain-agnostic—equally effective whether analyzing AI markets, mobile developer ecosystems, energy grids, or biotech supply chains.

---

## 1. Taxonomic Categories

*   **Black Swan:** A severe, structural disruption that was genuinely low-probability and un-executable even to informed industry insiders. It completely invalidates the market's baseline operational mathematics.
*   **Gray Rhino:** A highly telegraphed, high-probability crisis that was actively tracked and prepared for by informed insiders, but systematically ignored, downplayed, or mispriced by the broader market until it detonated.
*   **Gray Swan:** A known possibility driven by predictable architectural mechanics, but possessing highly volatile, un-forecastable timing, magnitude, or trigger conditions.
*   **Broken Prior (Non-Event):** A highly anticipated, catastrophic structural disruption that *failed* to materialize, breaking the market's defensive hedges and invalidating expensive risk-reversal strategies.
*   **Neither (Standard Volatility / Stress Test):** An isolated, localized, or transient event. It may cause intense near-term narrative drama or asset price fluctuations, but it fails to permanently alter market-wide strategic direction.

---

## 2. Ordered Decision Procedure (Step-by-Step Checks)

To classify any candidate event, pass it through the following gatekeeper checks in sequence:
### Check 1: The Topology Shift Test (Impact & Breadth)
*   **Question A:** Did this event break the baseline operational or financial assumptions of the ecosystem, forcing a meaningful share of market players (not just the entities directly involved) to fundamentally change their strategic direction?
*   **Question B:** Did it structurally rearrange the competitive leaderboard (permanently changing who is ahead, who is behind, and who is relevant at all)?
*   **Decision:** 
    *   If **YES** to both, proceed to **Check 2**.
    *   If **NO** to either, run this sub-check: *Was this a massively anticipated structural disruption that failed to materialize, breaking the defensive hedges of players who spent capital preparing for it?* If **YES**, classify as a **Broken Prior**. If **NO**, classify as **Neither (Standard Volatility)**.

### Check 2: The Horizon Test (Insider Foresight)
*   **Question:** Was the event explicitly telegraphed, systematically documented, and actively hedged against by informed industry insiders years or months in advance (even if the general public or public markets completely mispriced it)?
*   **Decision:**
    *   If **YES**, stop here. Classify as a **Gray Rhino**.
    *   If **NO**, proceed to **Check 3**.

### Check 3: The Architectural Mechanics Test (Probability Type)
*   **Question:** Was the underlying capability or technical/legal mechanism structurally anticipated as a long-term scientific or economic certainty, with only the precise timing, interface design, or adoption velocity acting as the surprise?
*   **Decision:**
    *   If **YES**, stop here. Classify as a **Gray Swan**.
    *   If **NO**, stop here. Classify as a **Black Swan**.

## 3. Concrete Benchmark Examples

### Positive Benchmark: DeepSeek R1 (January 2025)
*   **Classification:** Black Swan
*   **Check 1 (Topology Shift):** **PASSED.** It completely collapsed the cost-floor for frontier reasoning, forcing every major US lab to slash API pricing by up to 90%. It permanently destroyed the narrative that massive, multi-billion-dollar brute-force hardware CapEx was an impenetrable software moat.
*   **Check 2 (Horizon Test):** **FAILED.** While deep learning efficiency gains were expected, the specific deployment of pure Reinforcement Learning to achieve o1-parity for a stated cost of $5.6 million completely blindsided frontier lab executives and public equity markets.
*   **Check 3 (Architectural Mechanics):** **FAILED.** It broke the linear "scale-equals-intelligence" scaling law assumption that had governed the previous three years of global technology investment.

### Negative Benchmark: Epic v. Apple (2021 Onwards)
*   **Classification:** Gray Rhino
*   **Check 1 (Topology Shift):** **PASSED.** It fractured the unassailable aura of the iOS Walled Garden, forced the architectural creation of alternative payment and steering mechanisms, and reset mobile gaming monetization models.
*   **Check 2 (Horizon Test):** **PASSED.** The antitrust pressure had been compounding for years. Spotify’s formal EU complaint in 2019 and the public congressional antitrust hearings in 2020 laid out the exact structural grievances. Insiders knew a major corporate detonation was inevitable; the broader market simply chose to enjoy uncompressed margins until the stunt landed.

## 4. Analysis of Edge Cases

### Edge Case 1: The OpenAI Board Crisis / Sam Altman Firing (Nov 2023)
*   **Initial Presentation:** Appeared to be an absolute Black Swan. The world's leading AI company nearly vaporized over a single weekend due to a bizarre corporate governance structure.
*   **The Framework Test:** 
    *   *Check 1 (Topology Shift):* **FAILED.** While it caused immense narrative drama and temporary operational panic for Microsoft, it ultimately **failed** to rearrange the competitive leaderboard. Sam Altman was reinstated within five days, OpenAI maintained its market dominance, and competitors gained short-term API diversification trials rather than a permanent structural advantage. The baseline mathematical equations governing AI scaling and deployment were untouched.
*   **Final Classification:** Neither (High-Drama Governance Stress Test)

### Edge Case 2: Meta Publisher Class Action Lawsuit (May 2026)
*   **Initial Presentation:** A massive legal shock. Five of the world’s largest publishers filed a unified class action targeting Mark Zuckerberg personally for the intentional torrenting and seeding of millions of copyrighted books.
*   **The Framework Test:**
    *   *Check 1 (Topology Shift):* **FAILED (Contextual Boundary).** While the legal discovery is deeply damaging to Meta's reputation and creates significant financial volatility, it has **not** forced a market-wide strategic redirection. Why? Because the broader market had already priced in this legal risk. Following the New York Times v. OpenAI suit in late 2023, every major frontier lab had already systematically shifted their strategies toward aggressive synthetic data generation and multi-million dollar institutional data licensing agreements. This is localized compliance exposure, not a topology-reshaper.
*   **Final Classification:** Neither (Localized Legal Volatility)

### Edge Case 3: The Chip Export Controls Sequence (October 2022 – March 2025)
*   **Initial Presentation:** A cascading series of global trade restrictions that frequently wiped out tens of billions in semiconductor market cap (such as ASML's 16% single-day drop).
*   **The Framework Test:**
    *   *Check 1 (Topology Shift):* **PASSED.** It completely rewired global hardware supply chains, instituted a physical compute ceiling for an entire geographic superpower, and established compute as a sovereign geopolitical asset class.
    *   *Check 2 (Horizon Test):* **PASSED.** The escalation was systematically telegraphed. The Trump-era crippling of Huawei laid out the exact legislative blueprint. The passing of the CHIPS Act in August 2022 explicitly broadcasted the US transition to an aggressive industrial containment policy. Chinese actors actively prepared for it by executing massive pre-deadline hardware stockpiles, building smuggling networks, and launching a state-backed self-sufficiency drive.
*   **Strategic Insight (The Shock Cascade):** This is a textbook **Gray Rhino**. However, its structural aftermath became the exact pressure-cooker that forced Chinese engineers to innovate around hardware limitations, directly functioning as the core precursor signal that birthed the **DeepSeek Black Swan**.
*   **Final Classification:** Gray Rhino (Which parented a subsequent Black Swan)
