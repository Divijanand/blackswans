# Repository Re-Classification Report (New Framework Lens)

This file documents the manual re-classification of our historical event repository against the updated two-pronged decision procedure: **Topology Shift (Strategic Realignment + Leaderboard Reshuffle)** and **Insider Horizon/Mechanics**.

---

## 1. DeepSeek R1 (January 2025)
* **Rethink Direction?** Yes. It forced every US frontier lab to immediately slash API pricing and aggressively pivot toward distillation, reasoning models, and cost-efficiency over brute-force scaling.
* **Reshuffle Leaderboard?** Yes. It shattered the proprietary pricing moat of US labs and instantly vaulted Chinese open-weight models to capability parity.
* **Classification:** **Black Swan** (The Clean Positive)

## 2. Chip Export Controls (October 2022 – March 2025)
* **Rethink Direction?** Yes. It rewired global hardware procurement networks and forced a multi-billion-dollar sovereign self-sufficiency drive in China.
* **Reshuffle Leaderboard?** Yes. It artificially restricted Chinese hardware integrators while reinforcing the infrastructure dominance of US cloud hyperscalers.
* **Classification:** **Gray Rhino** (The trajectory and policy escalation were heavily telegraphed; Chinese firms explicitly hedged via massive hardware stockpiles and smuggling vectors months in advance).

## 3. NVIDIA Blackwell Scarcity Shock (2024–2026)
* **Rethink Direction?** Yes. It forced smaller labs to restructure software pipelines around "compute poverty" and hyperscalers to drastically alter multi-year CapEx budgets.
* **Reshuffle Leaderboard?** Yes. It locked out lower-capitalized foundational model competitors while guaranteeing record-high software-like margins for NVIDIA.
* **Classification:** **Gray Rhino** (A supply squeeze transitioning a highly telegraphed hardware generation is a known architectural constraint; Tier-1 buyers systematically prepared via massive pre-payments).

## 4. n8n Valuation Cascade (2025)
* **Rethink Direction?** Yes. It solidified the enterprise shift from simple conversational chatbots to autonomous, multi-step agentic orchestration middleware.
* **Reshuffle Leaderboard?** Yes. It rapidly elevated open-source workflow orchestrators over rigid, legacy linear automation software (e.g., Zapier).
* **Classification:** **Gray Swan** (The technology transition to agentic workflows was mechanically anticipated by developers; the shock was purely the hyper-compressed 7-month, 7x private valuation velocity).

## 5. Epic v. Apple (2021 Onwards)
* **Rethink Direction?** Yes. It catalyzed the architectural creation of the alternative "App2Web" commerce and external check-out processing pipeline.
* **Reshuffle Leaderboard?** Yes. It fractured Apple’s absolute 30% margin control, forcing alternative tiered fee structures and alternative marketplaces.
* **Classification:** **Gray Rhino** (The Clean Negative; Spotify filed its formal EU complaint in 2019, and explicit congressional antitrust hearings in 2020 thoroughly telegraphed the looming legal detonation).

## 6. OpenAI Board Crisis / Sam Altman Firing (November 2023)
* **Rethink Direction?** No. While it generated intense short-term media and corporate drama, the macroeconomic scaling trajectories and infrastructure roadmaps of the market were untouched.
* **Reshuffle Leaderboard?** No. Sam Altman was reinstated within five days, and OpenAI maintained its market-leading position.
* **Classification:** **Neither / High-Drama Governance Stress Test**

## 7. Meta Publisher Class Action Lawsuit (May 2026)
* **Rethink Direction?** No. By mid-2026, the broader generative AI market had already altered its data strategy toward synthetic generation and licensed institutional datasets following the initial 2023 New York Times litigation.
* **Reshuffle Leaderboard?** No. It functions as a severe corporate financial and compliance penalty for Meta, not an industry-wide capability or market-share reset.
* **Classification:** **Neither / Localized Legal Exposure**
