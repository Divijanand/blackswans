## Event 1: ChatGPT Launch (November 2022) — The GPT Moment

### 1. Vectors Hit
* **Tech Discontinuity:** Deep architectural realization of conversational Large Language Models (LLMs) productized via Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF).
* **Financial:** Triggers a massive global capital reallocation, draining capital from web3/crypto and legacy enterprise software into generative AI compute and infrastructure.
* **Geopolitical:** Ignites a structural "AI arms race" primarily between the US and China, shifting national security priorities toward compute containment and algorithmic sovereignty.

### 2. Key Actors Affected
* **Felt it first:** Knowledge workers (copywriters, junior developers, educators) and OpenAI’s immediate hyper-scaler partner, Microsoft, which moved instantly to capitalize on the exclusive deployment rights.
* **Felt it hardest:** Legacy search incumbents—specifically Google, which was forced into a corporate "Code Red"—and ed-tech platforms like Chegg, which saw immediate, catastrophic contractions in subscriber growth.
* **Insulated:** Legacy infrastructure providers and pure-play hardware manufacturers (NVIDIA). They were completely insulated from the software alignment panic because *everyone*, regardless of who won the application layer, had to buy their chips.

### 3. Precursor Signals
* **GPT-3 (2020):** Proved that massive scale yields emergent text generation capabilities, but it lacked user-friendly utility without complex prompt engineering.
* **InstructGPT (January 2022):** OpenAI’s low-key deployment of models trained with RLHF. This directly demonstrated that aligning models to user intent, rather than just predicting the next token, radically unlocked usability.
* **The Chinchilla Scaling Laws (March 2022):** DeepMind proved that existing models were severely undertrained relative to compute budgets, signaling that massive optimization efficiency was sitting on the table.

### 4. Shock Duration
* **Months to Years.** The acute *narrative shock* lasted approximately 4 months until the release of GPT-4 in March 2023. However, the structural *macroeconomic equilibrium*—realigning labor markets, corporate tech stacks, and legal definitions of intellectual property—is still being figured out today.

### 5. Recurrence & Swan Classification
* **Grey Swan.** By Nassim Taleb's strict criteria, a pure Black Swan must be an absolute surprise to everyone. For deep-learning researchers and industry insiders tracking transformer architectures since 2017, the capabilities were a known trajectory. The true shock was a *product design and adoption velocity* surprise—the interface friction dropping to zero, leading to 100 million users in two months.
