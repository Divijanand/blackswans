## Event 10: GT Advanced Technologies Bankruptcy (Oct 2014) — The R&D Collapse

### 1. Vectors Hit
* **Supply-Chain:** A catastrophic failure of physical materials science scaling. Apple attempted to replace Corning Gorilla Glass with scratch-proof synthetic Sapphire for the iPhone 6 screen, requiring GTAT to produce 262-kilogram sapphire "boules" (a size never before achieved in human history).
* **Financial:** A brutal lesson in predatory corporate structuring. Apple advanced GTAT $578 million to build the furnaces, but the contract allegedly didn't actually obligate Apple to *buy* any of the sapphire if it didn't meet impossible specs. GTAT burned through the cash, failed the yield rates, and filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy overnight.
* **Tech Discontinuity:** Instantly killed Apple's multi-year R&D hardware roadmap. Apple was forced to execute a frantic, last-minute pivot back to Corning Gorilla Glass just weeks before the iPhone 6 entered mass production.

### 2. Key Actors Affected
* **Felt it first:** GTAT shareholders. The stock plummeted from over $11 to $0.80 overnight (a 93% wipeout), completely destroying a publicly traded company.
* **Felt it hardest:** Apple's hardware supply-chain executives. They lost hundreds of millions in the failed factory and suffered a rare, highly public embarrassment regarding their supply-chain omnipotence.
* **Insulated / Beneficiaries:** Corning. Written off by the market as a casualty of Apple's sapphire pivot, Corning instantly regained its monopoly on smartphone glass when GTAT collapsed.

### 3. Precursor Signals
* **Physics Limitations:** Independent materials scientists spent 2013 warning that while sapphire is harder than glass, it is substantially more *brittle*. Scaling it to smartphone sizes without it shattering during drop-tests was a known, massive physical hurdle.
* **GTAT's Previous Scale:** GTAT had never actually produced sapphire at this scale before; they previously just sold the *equipment* to make it. Apple basically forced an equipment vendor to become the largest materials manufacturer on earth in less than 12 months.

### 4. Shock Duration
* **Instantaneous (Financial) / Permanent (R&D).** GTAT vanished, and Apple never attempted to put a pure sapphire screen on an iPhone again, relegating the material permanently to small use-cases like Apple Watch faces and camera lenses.

### 5. Recurrence & Swan Classification
* **Grey Swan.** Suppliers failing to meet Apple's demanding specs is common, but a publicly traded company taking half a billion dollars from Apple and going totally bankrupt mid-production is a massive, localized Black Swan for hardware R&D.
